Winter Olympic Game

The first organized international competition involving winter sports was introduced just five years after the birth of the modern Olympics in 1896. Known as the Nordic Games, this competition included athletes predominantly from Nordic countries (such as Norway and Sweden).

Few years later an agreement was reached to celebrate an IOC-sanctioned International Winter Sports Week. Held in Chamonix, France, in 1924, the event was a huge success.

CANADA's each Winter Olympic Games performance

  • Canada (IOC country code CAN) has competed at every Winter Olympic Games, and has won at least one medal each time. By total medals, the country's best performance was in the 2014 Winter Olympic Games where Canadian athletes won 90 medals in total.
  • From the line chart, there are three clear trends in the past 22 Olympic games. First, a smooth and steady trend from 1924-1968, there is a slight float but overall is in the initial development stage.
  • The second period is 1972-1988. Look at that trend and the medal during that period are very, very low. Magnussen’s medal was Canada’s only podium result of the Games, making the Sapporo Games the Canada's poorest medal showing at a Winter Olympics since 1924.

Detailed analysis of 2010 and 2014

Can you imagine that how welL Canada done in 2010 and 2014 these two years?

At the 2010 Winter Olympics, which was hosted in Vancouver, British Columbia. Canada won more gold medals than any other competing nation for the first time and more than quintupled the five medals it won in Calgary at the 2010 Winter Olympics also.

Canada set a new record for most gold medals won by a country in a single Winter Olympics with 14 at the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, Canada. This achievement surpassed the previous record of 13 gold medals held by the Soviet Union (1976) and Norway (2002).
